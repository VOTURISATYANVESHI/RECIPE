Onion Samosa Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

Procedure:

  1. Prepare the dough by combining all-purpose flour, vegetable oil, carom seeds, and salt. Add water as needed to make a firm, smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Saut√© until the onions turn translucent.
  4. Stir in red chili powder, garam masala, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are well incorporated and the mixture becomes dry. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  5. Divide the rested dough into small equal-sized balls and roll each ball into a thin oval-shaped sheet.
  6. Cut the oval-shaped sheet into two equal halves to make two semi-circles.
  7. Take one semi-circle, fold it into a cone shape, and seal the edges using a little water to make a cone.
  8. Fill the cone with the prepared onion filling and seal the open edge to form a triangular samosa shape.
  9. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Fry the samosas until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  10. Remove the fried samosas from the oil and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  11. Serve hot with your favorite chutney or sauce.
