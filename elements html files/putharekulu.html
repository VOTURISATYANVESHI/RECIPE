Pootharekulu Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 10-15 rice papers (edible transparent sheets)
  • 1/2 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 2 tablespoons cashews, chopped (optional)
  • A pinch of salt

Procedure:

  1. Heat ghee in a pan over low heat.
  2. Add rice flour and roast it in the ghee until it turns slightly golden and gives off a pleasant aroma. Keep stirring to avoid burning. This may take about 4-5 minutes.
  3. Remove the roasted rice flour from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. In a separate mixing bowl, combine powdered sugar, cardamom powder, chopped cashews (if using), and a pinch of salt.
  5. Add the cooled roasted rice flour to the sugar mixture and mix well to combine. Ensure that there are no lumps.
  6. Take one rice paper sheet and place it on a clean, dry surface.
  7. Sprinkle a thin layer of the rice flour-sugar mixture evenly over the rice paper sheet.
  8. Place another rice paper sheet on top of the mixture and press gently to stick them together.
  9. Repeat the process, alternating between rice paper sheets and the mixture until you have a stack of several layers.
  10. Using a sharp knife, trim the edges of the stack to form a neat rectangle or square shape.
  11. Cut the stack into smaller rectangular pieces or desired shapes.
  12. Your Pootharekulu are ready to be served. Enjoy this traditional Andhra sweet delicacy!
