Chapati Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • Water (for kneading)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (optional)

Procedure:

  1. In a mixing bowl, add the whole wheat flour and, if desired, salt.
  2. Gradually add water while kneading the flour to form a soft, smooth, and non-sticky dough. The amount of water may vary, so add it little by little until you achieve the right consistency.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes. This allows the gluten to relax and makes rolling the chapatis easier.
  4. Divide the dough into small, equal-sized portions and shape them into round balls. You can make them slightly larger than a golf ball.
  5. Take one dough ball and roll it into a smooth ball between your palms. Flatten it slightly to form a small disk.
  6. Using a rolling pin, roll out the dough ball into a thin, round chapati. You can dust the rolling surface with a little dry flour to prevent sticking.
  7. Heat a flat griddle or tava over medium-high heat. Once hot, place the rolled chapati on the griddle and cook for about 20-30 seconds on one side until small bubbles start to appear.
  8. Flip the chapati and cook the other side for about 20-30 seconds until it puffs up and develops golden brown spots.
  9. Remove the cooked chapati from the griddle and keep it warm by wrapping it in a clean cloth or aluminum foil while you cook the remaining chapatis.
  10. Your homemade Chapatis (Rotis) are ready to be served! Enjoy them with your favorite curry or side dish.
