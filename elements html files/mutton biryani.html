Mutton Biryani Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4-6 cloves
  • 4-6 green cardamom pods
  • 2-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Procedure:

  1. Wash and soak the basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat.
  3. Add bay leaves, cloves, green cardamom, and cinnamon. Sauté until fragrant.
  4. Add thinly sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  7. Add mutton pieces and cook until they turn brown.
  8. Stir in turmeric powder, red chili powder, biryani masala, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are well incorporated.
  9. Add yogurt and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
  10. Meanwhile, in a separate pot, bring water to a boil and cook the soaked basmati rice until it is 70-80% cooked. Drain the rice and set aside.
  11. Layer the partially cooked rice over the mutton mixture in the pot.
  12. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and place it on a low flame. Cook for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and the flavors are infused.
  13. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  14. Serve hot with raita or salad.
