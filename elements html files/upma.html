Upma Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup semolina (rava or sooji)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (black gram)
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, bell peppers, etc.), finely chopped
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2.5 cups water
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)
  • Lemon juice (optional)

Procedure:

  1. Dry Roasting Semolina:
    • Heat a pan or kadai over medium heat.
    • Add the semolina (rava) and dry roast it while stirring continuously. Roast until it turns light golden brown and you can smell a nutty aroma. This step takes about 5-7 minutes. Be careful not to over-roast, as it can become too dry.
  2. Remove and Set Aside:
    • Remove the roasted semolina from the pan and transfer it to a plate. Set it aside.
  3. Tempering:
    • In the same pan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat.
    • Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add urad dal and chana dal. Sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add Aromatics:
    • Add cumin seeds, grated ginger, and finely chopped green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the ginger is fragrant.
  5. Onion and Veggies:
    • Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  6. Add Vegetables and Curry Leaves:
    • Add the mixed vegetables and curry leaves. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the vegetables start to soften.
  7. Add Asafoetida and Roasted Semolina:
    • Add asafoetida (hing) and the roasted semolina to the pan. Mix everything well.
  8. Boiling Water:
    • Pour 2.5 cups of water into the pan while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  9. Cooking Upma:
    • Reduce the heat to low and cover the pan. Let the upma cook for 3-4 minutes until the semolina absorbs the water and becomes soft and fluffy. Stir occasionally.
  10. Adjust Consistency:
    • If the upma appears too dry, you can add a little more hot water and mix until you achieve the desired consistency.
  11. Season with Salt:
    • Season the upma with salt to taste and mix well.
  12. Garnish and Serve:
    • Optionally, garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a drizzle of lemon juice for extra flavor.
  13. Serve Hot:
    • Serve the upma hot with coconut chutney, pickle, or yogurt.
