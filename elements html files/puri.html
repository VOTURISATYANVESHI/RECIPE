Poori Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • Water (for kneading)
  • Oil for deep frying

Procedure:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt. Mix well.
  2. Add ghee or oil to the flour mixture and rub it in with your fingertips until it resembles bread crumbs.
  3. Gradually add water while kneading the flour to form a smooth and firm dough. Knead for about 5 minutes until the dough is soft and elastic. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small, equal-sized portions and shape them into round balls. Roll each ball between your palms to make them smooth.
  5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium-high heat.
  6. Take one dough ball and flatten it slightly with your fingertips. Roll it out into a small circle, about 4-5 inches in diameter. The poori should not be too thin or too thick.
  7. Gently slide the rolled poori into the hot oil and press it lightly with a slotted spoon. It should puff up. Fry until it turns golden brown on both sides, turning it as needed.
  8. Remove the fried poori from the oil using a slotted spoon and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Repeat the process for the remaining dough balls.
  9. Your delicious Pooris are ready to be served! Enjoy them with your favorite side dishes like potato masala or chana masala.
