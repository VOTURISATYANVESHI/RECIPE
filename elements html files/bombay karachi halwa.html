Bombay Karachi Halwa Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup corn flour
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 cup mixed nuts (cashews, almonds, pistachios), chopped
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • A few drops of food coloring (orange or pink, optional)

Procedure:

  1. In a mixing bowl, dissolve corn flour in 1 cup of water to make a smooth, lump-free mixture. Set it aside.
  2. Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan over low-medium heat.
  3. Add the dissolved corn flour mixture to the ghee and stir continuously to avoid lumps. Cook the mixture until it thickens and starts to leave the sides of the pan. This may take about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Stir in sugar and continue to cook, stirring continuously, until the mixture thickens and leaves the sides of the pan again. This may take another 7-10 minutes.
  5. Add cardamom powder and saffron strands (if using) to the mixture and mix well.
  6. If you want to add food coloring, divide the mixture into two equal portions and add a few drops of the desired color to each portion. Mix well to evenly color the halwa.
  7. Add the chopped nuts to the mixture and mix to distribute them evenly.
  8. Grease a square or rectangular tray with ghee or line it with parchment paper.
  9. Pour the colored halwa mixture (if using food coloring) into the tray, spreading it evenly.
  10. Allow the halwa to cool and set for a few hours. Once set, cut it into squares or diamonds using a sharp knife.
  11. Your Bombay Karachi Halwa is ready to be served. Enjoy!
