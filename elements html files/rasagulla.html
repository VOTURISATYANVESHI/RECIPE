Rasgulla Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup lemon juice or white vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few saffron strands (optional)

Procedure:

  1. Boil the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan, stirring continuously to prevent it from sticking to the bottom.
  2. Once the milk comes to a boil, reduce the heat and add lemon juice or white vinegar gradually while stirring. The milk will curdle, and whey will separate from the curdled milk solids.
  3. Turn off the heat and strain the curdled milk through a muslin cloth or cheesecloth to separate the chenna (paneer) from the whey. Rinse the chenna under cold running water to remove any residual lemon or vinegar flavor.
  4. Gather the cloth with the chenna and squeeze out excess water. Hang the chenna bundle for about 30 minutes to drain any remaining water.
  5. After draining, transfer the chenna to a clean surface and knead it with the heel of your hand until it becomes smooth and soft. This may take about 5-7 minutes.
  6. Divide the kneaded chenna into equal-sized portions and shape them into smooth balls without any cracks. Ensure there are no cracks, or the rasgullas may break during cooking.
  7. In a large saucepan, combine sugar, water, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using). Bring the sugar syrup to a boil.
  8. Gently add the chenna balls (rasgullas) to the boiling sugar syrup. Cover the saucepan with a lid and cook on medium heat for about 15-20 minutes.
  9. The rasgullas will expand in size as they cook and absorb the sugar syrup. They should double in size and become spongy.
  10. Turn off the heat and let the rasgullas cool in the sugar syrup for a few hours. As they cool, they will soak up more syrup and become even softer.
  11. Your delicious Rasgullas are ready to be served! You can refrigerate them and enjoy them chilled.
